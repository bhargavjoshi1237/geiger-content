# Geiger Content Market Research

Research date: June 12, 2026

## Goal

Build a feature and pricing map for a modern Content OS, then manually choose the combination that gives Geiger Content the strongest relevance at an accessible price.

### 3. Publishing Management

- Draft and published APIs
- Dev, staging and production environments

## Market Rarity Scale

### Very Common

Available in most modern CMS products, often free or inexpensive:

- Structured content and reusable components
- REST or GraphQL delivery
- Roles and basic permissions
- Localization
- Preview, webhooks and version history
- Asset management
- Basic AI writing assistance

### Common Paid

Usually included in team plans or mid-market packages:

- Comments, tasks and collaboration
- Scheduled publishing
- Multiple environments
- Visual page editing
- Releases and rollback
- Custom roles
- Usage reporting

### Advanced

Common in enterprise CMS or DXP products, but rarely affordable:

- Custom workflows and granular governance
- Taxonomy management
- Content variants and audience segments
- Component-level personalization
- A/B testing and experience analytics
- Unified DAM and content operations
- Real-time profile integrations

### Rare

Usually supplied by separate CDP, experimentation or recommendation products:

- Native known and anonymous user profiles
- Identity resolution
- Real-time behavioral segments
- Edge personalization and decisioning
- Holdouts and multi-armed bandits
- Personalized search and recommendations
- Cross-channel journey orchestration

### Extremely Rare

Potential Geiger Content differentiators:

- A user has a separate lifecycle stage for every topic
- `User -> Topic -> Stage -> Recommended Content`
- Semantic content-consumption knowledge graph
- Editorially controlled recommendation ranking
- Explainable reasons for each content decision
- CMS, lightweight CDP, testing and recommendations in one affordable product
- Price based on useful scale rather than locking intelligence behind enterprise contracts

## Initial Product Opportunity

Do not compete by offering more basic CMS features. That market is mature and inexpensive.

Geiger Content should combine:

1. Strong structured content and editorial workflow
2. Built-in content slots, tags, variants and audience rules
3. Lightweight user profiles and behavior events
4. User-topic stages and explainable targeting
5. A/B testing with goals and holdouts
6. Semantic recommendations with editorial controls
7. Fast SSR and edge delivery

This combination is normally assembled from several products or purchased through a custom enterprise DXP contract.

## Suggested Relevance Scoring

Score every candidate feature from 1 to 5:

- **Need:** How often target customers require it
- **Rarity:** How uncommon it is at non-enterprise prices
- **Differentiation:** How strongly it supports the Geiger Content idea
- **Build cost:** Engineering and operational difficulty
- **Price leverage:** Whether customers will pay more for it

Priority score:

`Need + Rarity + Differentiation + Price Leverage - Build Cost`
